Research Interests

I am interested in the psychosocial factors that shape the production of constitutive value and how this comes to be reified and projected onto digital mediums. For instance, examining what kinds of knowledge and community are produced and how they are formed allows us to understand the values and fears that drive society and our lived experience. I study technology by analyzing how humans extend such behaviors to digital mediums. From the construction of computational language, tools, and systems to how they are engaged en masse, I hope to explore its materialization on a computational and social level. To me, this is to think through developmental practices in design and how its efficacy is measured. This is also to think through what such products assume about subjectivity, where I hope to bridge forms of social theory that I consider interesting and important to bring to the discussion.


Tentatively, I hope to ground this endeavor within the intersection of affect and computing.

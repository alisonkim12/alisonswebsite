motivation for thesis inquiry

Computational technology has produced some of the most harmful and destructive outcomes in the world, yet the discipline is not portrayed or treated with the seriousness it warrants. Students learning to code and creating computational technology products often do so without understanding these critical issues. Considering imperial dynamics, Western capitalist companies develop and use technologies to profit from and exploit populations in the third world/global south. Technologies are increasingly used as mediums, and even arbiters, of decision-making, yet the implications of this are not deeply considered by those in the field of computer science. If educational institutions do not provide the opportunity for computer scientists to learn and understand these issues, it is unclear where they will gain this crucial knowledge. While it may be difficult to enforce repercussions for ignorance, there is a moral obligation to understand these ethical concerns. The repercussions of computer scientists lacking this education are not formally measured, but we can clearly see the consequences with major tech conglomerates producing weapons of mass destruction, exploiting and outsourcing labor to the global south, and unethically mining user data. There is a significant dissonance between the abstraction of computer science development and its real-world applications, which should be deeply concerning to everyone.
